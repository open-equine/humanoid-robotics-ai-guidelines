## Ground Rules

**Rule 1 — Humanoids, AI and Robots are not a replacement for human staff.**

**Rule 2 — No exception overrides Rule 1.**

## A few important principles derive from these Ground Rules.

1. The horse has priority over operational efficiency at all times.
2. Human authority over stable decisions is non-delegable.
3. Technology enters a stable on the stable's terms — not the manufacturer's.
4. Veterinary authority over health, medication, and emergency decisions is absolute.
5. Humans must be able to view the source code of machines operating in the stable environment.
6. Humans retain control over the operations of all machines in the stable environment.

---

## Ground Rules — Detail

**Rule 1 — Humanoids, AI and Robots are not a replacement for human staff.**
Every stable operation that deploys any technology covered under this framework must maintain its full human staffing complement. Technology is a tool of support. Reduction of human staff on the basis of technology deployment is prohibited.

**Rule 2 — No exception overrides Rule 1.**
No operational, commercial, or technical justification permits a reduction in human presence in a stable environment as a consequence of deploying humanoid machines, AI systems, or robotic equipment. This rule is absolute.

---

## Derived Principles — Detail

**Principle 1 — The horse has priority over operational efficiency at all times.**
In any conflict between the welfare of a horse and the efficiency of a stable operation, the welfare of the horse takes precedence. No technology system, workflow, or schedule may override this principle.

**Principle 2 — Human authority over stable decisions is non-delegable.**
All decisions affecting the health, safety, and welfare of horses remain exclusively with qualified human personnel. No AI output, robotic action, or humanoid decision constitutes a substitute for human judgment in a stable environment.

**Principle 3 — Technology enters a stable on the stable's terms — not the manufacturer's.**
No technology system — regardless of its commercial designation, manufacturer claims, or stated capabilities — may be deployed in a stable environment without prior assessment against the safety guidelines of that stable. The stable sets the terms. The machine adapts.

**Principle 4 — Veterinary authority over health, medication, and emergency decisions is absolute.**
All health, medication, and emergency decisions belong exclusively to qualified veterinary professionals. No technology system may act on, recommend, or substitute for veterinary authority.

**Principle 5 — Humans must be able to view the source code of machines operating in the stable environment.**
Any machine operating in a stable environment must have its source code visible and accessible to the stable management. A machine whose logic cannot be inspected cannot be trusted in proximity to horses and humans.

**Principle 6 — Humans retain control over the operations of all machines in the stable environment.**
No machine operating in a stable environment may take action that is beyond the knowledge or override capability of the human personnel present. Human control over stable operations is mandatory. It is the condition under which any machine is permitted to operate.
